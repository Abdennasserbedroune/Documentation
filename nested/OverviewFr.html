Beewant Account Management

This section explains how to set up, customize, and secure your Beewant account. It walks through each relevant area of the Settings menu with clear, practical instructions.

Getting Started

You can create a Beewant account using:

  • Google (Gmail)
  • Microsoft
  • Email verification

Follow the steps below based on your preferred method.

TIP: Choose the authentication method that aligns with your organization's security policies. SSO (Single Sign-On) may be available for Enterprise accounts, which simplifies account management across teams.

Creating an Account with Google or Microsoft

  1. Go to the registration page Open the Beewant login screen and select Continue with Google or Continue with Microsoft.
  2. Sign in with your account If you are not already logged in, authenticate with your Google or Microsoft credentials.
  3. Automatic account creation After successful authentication, you will be redirected back to Beewant and your account will be created automatically.

Creating an Account with Email

  1. Go to the registration page On the Beewant signup screen, select Continue with Email.
  2. Request a verification code Enter your email address and submit the form. Beewant will send a verification code to your inbox.
  3. Retrieve the code Open your email inbox, locate the message from Beewant, and copy the verification code.
  4. Confirm your email Paste or type the verification code into the registration form to confirm your email and complete account creation.
If you do not receive the code:
  • Check your spam or junk folder.
  • Click Send again on the registration form to request a new code.
  • Confirm that you entered the correct email address.

Profile & Personal Information

The Profile tab in the sidebar lets you view and manage your basic account details.

You can:

  • Update your name, username.
  • Add a profile photo (PNG or JPEG, up to 1 MB, recommended size: 1200 × 1200 px).
  • Manage your contact information, including phone number and address (country, city, region).
BEST PRACTICE: Use a professional profile photo and keep your contact information current to ensure team members and administrators can reach you when needed.

Beewant Account Management Security Credentials

Security & Passwords

Use the Security & Access section to manage how you sign in and protect your account.

You can:

  • Change your password at any time.
  • Reset your password if you forget your login details.
  • Access the Danger Zone to permanently delete your account.
WARNING: Deleting your account is irreversible. This action removes all your data and active subscriptions. Only proceed if you are certain you no longer need access to Beewant.
BEST PRACTICE: Change your password every 90 days and use a strong, unique password containing uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and special characters. Never share your password with others.

API Access (Pro & Enterprise only)

The API Access tab is available for Pro and Enterprise customers.

With API access, you can:

  • Generate API tokens to enable secure, programmatic access to your Beewant workspace. This is ideal for integrations, internal tools, or automation workflows.
  • Copy and rotate tokens with a single click whenever your security policies or workflows change.
SECURITY RECOMMENDATION: Treat API tokens like passwords. Do not share them publicly, embed them in client-side code, or commit them to version control repositories. If you suspect that a token has been exposed, revoke and regenerate it immediately.
TIP: Store API tokens in environment variables or secure secrets management systems (e.g., AWS Secrets Manager, HashiCorp Vault) rather than hardcoding them in your application.

Preferences & Personalization

In Preferences, you can tailor Beewant to your working style and environment. These settings help ensure that Beewant feels natural and efficient to use in your daily workflows.

You can:

  • Set your preferred interface language.
  • Adjust basic account behaviors and display options.
  • Manage your memories by enabling the memories feature, and you can manage and delete these memories; you can also disable this feature.
  • Switch between light mode and dark mode, so the interface matches your visual comfort and workplace lighting conditions.
TIP: Enable the Memory feature if you want Beewant to learn from previous conversations and provide more personalized responses. Disable it if you prefer fresh interactions without context carryover.

Plans, Billing & Subscription Management

The Billing section lets you manage your subscription and understand available options.

You can:

  • Compare all plans—from Free to Pro and Enterprise—and review their included features.
  • Upgrade or change plans at any time.
  • View your payment history and invoices for your records.

Enterprise customers have access to advanced capabilities such as SSO (Single Sign‑On), allowing users to sign in to Beewant with their existing corporate identity provider.

NOTE: SSO is configured by your organization's IT or security team. Once enabled, end users simply log in with their usual company credentials—no additional setup is required on their side.
TIP: Review your plan usage monthly to ensure you have the appropriate subscription tier for your team's needs.

Agents Expertise at Scale

Beewant Agents are specialized AI assistants designed to handle specific domains and use cases within the Beewant platform. They help teams automate repetitive workflows, analyze complex data, and deliver tailored recommendations with speed and consistency.

Each agent is configured with a clear focus such as financial analysis, contract review, or learning support so users receive precise, context-aware assistance for their tasks. Beewant Agents turn complex processes into simple, conversational experiences.

Why Use Agents?

  • Same behavior across multiple conversations
  • Domain-specific expertise (coding, support, analysis)
  • Consistent quality and tone
  • Easy to maintain and update
TIP: Agents are ideal for standardizing responses across teams. Use them to enforce consistent messaging, reduce hallucinations, and ensure quality control.

Creating an Agent

Go to Agents in the sidebar, click + Create Agent, fill in:

  • Name (50 characters max) — identifier for your agent
  • Description (200 characters max) — what this agent does
  • Instructions (50,000 characters max) — how the agent should behave
  • Preferred Model (optional) — default AI model to use

Note: Writing Good Instructions

Keep it structured and specific:

You are a data analyst expert.

Knowledge areas:
- SQL query optimization
- Python (pandas, NumPy)
- Data visualization

Behavior:
- Ask clarifying questions about data sources
- Explain technical concepts simply
- Suggest multiple approaches when relevant

Response style:
- Professional but approachable
- Include code examples for technical queries
- Use bullet points for key findings

Avoid:
- Assuming data quality without verification
- Over-complicating solutions

Better than: "Be a helpful data analyst."
BEST PRACTICE: Include at least 1,000+ characters in instructions to provide sufficient context and constraints. Specify what the agent should NOT do to minimize off-topic responses.
TIP: Use specific role definitions (e.g., "You are a legal compliance reviewer for enterprise contracts") rather than generic instructions. This reduces hallucination and improves relevance.

Using Agents in Chat

  1. Click the agent icon in the chat input bar
  2. Select an agent from the list
  3. Start typing—this agent's instructions are guides for all future replies.
NOTE: Changing agents mid-conversation only applies the new agent's instructions to future messages, not retroactively. Start a new chat if you want a fresh perspective from a different agent.

Customizing Pre-built Agents

  1. Find the agent on the Agents page
  2. Click Edit (pencil icon)
  3. Change name, description, instructions, or model
  4. Click Create to save as a personal copy
TIP: Always create a copy of pre-built agents before editing them. This preserves the original and allows you to revert if needed.

Managing Agents

Find agents:

  • Use the search bar to find by name
  • Filter by tab: All, My agents, Groups, Agents Public, Agents by Beewant.

Delete unused agents:

  • Click the menu (⋮) and select Delete
  • Keep your Agents library clean
TIP: Regularly audit your Agents library and delete outdated or underutilized agents to reduce clutter and improve discoverability.

Share Agents

Agents in Beewant can be shared, giving you flexibility between targeted collaboration and wider visibility. You can do that by clicking on (⋮) where you can find both options: invite members and publish agents.

  • You can generate a shareable link for an agent and send it via email, or you can copy a generated link and send it to specific members, so they can use it while it remains organized inside your group's Agents workspace.
  • You can also share an agent more broadly across Beewant, making it available to all users in the workspace so everyone can benefit from the same configured instructions.
TIP: Document your agent's purpose and usage guidelines (e.g., expected input format, typical response time, known limitations) when sharing widely to ensure consistent adoption.

Library & Collections Knowledge at Your Fingertips

Collections in Beewant enable users to group multiple indexed media such as documents, audio files, and videos into cohesive sets for seamless interactions. This feature streamlines content management by allowing users to manage, update, and retrieve related media as unified collections. Collections enhance collaborative and structured workflows, letting users leverage grouped content dynamically within chat, search, and agent sessions.

TIP: Use Collections to organize content by project, client, department, or time period. This makes retrieval faster and enables efficient batch analysis.

Supported Formats

Documents

  • PDF (text, image, or OCR) - Use PDFs to bring in reports, slide exports, contracts, or scanned documents. Beewant can read both digital text PDFs and scanned/image PDFs (once processed with OCR), allowing users to summarize and ask questions about the full content.
  • Word (DOCX, DOC) - Upload Word files for policies, proposals, briefs, and internal documentation. These files are ideal when teams frequently revise content, as Beewant can surface the latest version structure, headings, and body text in responses.
NOTE: Only the latest version of a file is indexed. If you update a file, re-upload it or use the Reindex button to ensure Beewant accesses the newest content.
  • PowerPoint (PPTX) - Use PowerPoint decks to analyze presentations, training materials, and client slideware. Beewant can work across slides to extract talking points, summarize sections, and answer questions about charts, bullet lists, and speaker notes.
  • Excel (XLSX) - Upload Excel files for structured data such as KPIs, financial models, logs, or tracking sheets. Beewant can help interpret tables, identify trends, and answer targeted questions about specific columns, rows, or metrics.
  • Markdown (MD) - Use Markdown files for technical documentation, changelogs, READMEs, and lightweight specs. These are especially useful for engineering teams, as Beewant preserves headings and structure to make navigation and querying more intuitive.

Code and Text

  • TXT - Plain text files are best for raw notes, transcripts, quick exports, or legacy content. They provide a simple way to bring unstructured information into Beewant for search and Q& A.
  • HTML - Upload HTML when you want to analyze web pages, internal portals, or exported app views. Beewant focuses on the readable text content, making it easier to work with page copy without needing to open a browser.
  • CSS - CSS files are useful when reviewing or documenting design systems and styling rules. Beewant can help explain selectors, variables, and layout behavior, or support front-end teams during reviews.
  • JavaScript - Use JavaScript files to document front-end logic, workflows, or small utilities. Beewant can assist with understanding code behavior, identifying key functions, and clarifying how parts of the script interact.
  • Python - Python files are ideal for data pipelines, scripts, and backend logic. Beewant can help interpret functions, modules, and workflows, making it easier for teams to onboard, refactor, or document existing code.
  • CSV - CSV files are suited to tabular exports from CRMs, analytics tools, or databases. Beewant can read and reason over rows and columns, helping users explore data, spot patterns, and answer specific data questions.
TIP: Use clear column headers and consistent formatting to improve analysis accuracy.
  • JSON - Use JSON for configuration files, API responses, logs, or structured app data. Beewant can parse nested structures, explain fields, and support debugging or documentation of your data models.

Media

  • MP4 (video) - Upload MP4 videos to work with recordings such as meetings, trainings, webinars, or demos. Beewant can help extract key moments, summarize content, and answer questions about what was discussed or shown.
NOTE: Videos are automatically transcribed. Transcription time depends on video length; allow several minutes for processing.
  • MP3 (audio) - Use MP3 files for voice notes, interviews, podcasts, or call recordings. Once processed, Beewant can surface themes, turn conversations into structured notes, and support follow-up questions on the audio content.
  • YouTube URLs - Paste YouTube links to analyze public videos without downloading them manually. Beewant can derive summaries, highlight important sections, and enable Q& A around the video's topics, making external content easier to reuse in your workflows.

Beewant's video and audio features turn recordings into searchable, reusable knowledge that is easy to review, edit, and export. These capabilities are designed to support workflows like meeting documentation, training analysis, interviews, and research.

Automatic Transcription

Every video and audio file uploaded to Beewant is automatically transcribed. The full text of the recording is indexed with timestamps, so you can quickly jump to specific moments and search for exact phrases across your media. Speaker turns are labeled (for example, SPEAKER_01, SPEAKER_02, etc.), making multi-speaker conversations easier to follow and analyze.

TIP: Search transcripts for specific keywords or time ranges to quickly locate relevant discussion points.

Customize Speaker Names

To make transcripts more readable and useful, you can rename speakers with meaningful labels. Open the video sidebar, click the gear icon in the video sidebar, then replace generic labels (such as SPEAKER_01) with real names like "John" or "Marketing Lead." After editing, click Save.

Download Transcriptions

Transcripts can be exported for use in reports, documentation, or external tools. From the video sidebar, click the download icon, choose your preferred format (TXT, JSON, or PDF), and then select Export. This gives you a portable copy of the transcription that can be archived, shared, or integrated into other systems as needed.

BEST PRACTICE: Export transcripts in JSON format if you plan to integrate them with external systems or perform automated analysis.

After renaming speakers in a transcript, reindexing ensures that the new names are fully applied to search and analysis.

Limits

  • Max 50 files per upload
  • Max 100MB per file
WARNING: If either of these limits is exceeded, the user receives a notification indicating that the upload cannot proceed until the number of files or file size is reduced to comply with the constraints. Batch uploads in multiple iterations if you have a large repository.

FAQ

Can I use multiple agents in one chat?

Yes. Switch agents anytime using the agent selector. Only future messages use the new agent.

Are my Library files private?

Yes, by default. You can invite specific people or make collections public.

What happens if I delete a chat?

Permanent deletion. Deleted chats cannot be recovered.

How do I ensure consistent agent behavior?

Write specific, detailed instructions. Test the agent with diverse queries. Update instructions based on performance.

Can I collaborate on Collections with my team?

Yes. Share collections with specific members via email invite or generate a shareable link. For broad access, publish the collection to your workspace.

How long does indexing take for large files?

Indexing depends on file size and type. Most files (under 100MB) are indexed within 2–5 minutes. Very large or complex files may take longer.

What should I do if transcription accuracy is poor?

Ensure audio quality is high (clear speech, minimal background noise). Re-upload with better audio if available, or manually edit the transcript using the speaker name customization feature.

How can I build a custom agent for my team's workflow?

  1. Identify the domain/expertise needed (e.g., "Financial Analysis")
  2. Define what the agent should know (knowledge areas, data sources)
  3. Specify how it should behave (tone, process, constraints)
  4. Test with sample queries before sharing widely
  5. Document expected inputs and outputs

What's the difference between agents and just using Beewant directly?

Agents encode your expertise, standards, and company knowledge so it's consistent, scalable, and hands-free. One request to a trained agent orchestrates multiple tools and applies your business logic automatically.

Examples and Use Cases

Example: "Procurement-ready vendor comparison"

Goal: A security/IT team wants to choose a vendor (e.g., a transcription provider) and needs a decision memo that's consistent with internal requirements and formatting standards.

Web Search Alone (Chat with Web Only)

User prompt: "Compare Vendor A vs Vendor B for enterprise transcription. Include pricing, security, and compliance."

What happens (typical outcome):

  • The assistant pulls fresh info from the web and summarizes it.
  • But the result often varies in structure and depth depending on how the user asks.
  • It may miss internal constraints (required compliance checklist, preferred decision template, how to label risk, the tone needed for leadership).

Output quality: Good summary, but not reliably "company-ready."

Agents + Web Search (Beewant Way)

Step 1 — Create a dedicated Agent

Create an agent with detailed instructions (structured and specific).

Agent name: "Procurement Analyst (Security & Compliance)" Agent instructions (example):

  • "Always produce a vendor comparison in the following structure: Executive summary → Requirements checklist → Risk notes → Recommendation."
  • "Always use Chat with Web for up-to-date vendor claims; never assume missing data—mark as 'Not found'."
  • "Ask 2 clarifying questions first: required compliance (SOC2/ISO), region (EU/US), and deployment constraints."
  • "Keep tone enterprise/procurement."

This is exactly what Agents are for: consistent behavior, domain-specific expertise, and repeatable quality across conversations.

Step 2 — Run one simple request

User prompt (simple): "Compare Vendor A vs Vendor B for enterprise transcription and recommend one."

What the Agent does automatically (key difference)

Because the Agent has rules, it orchestrates Web Search in a controlled way:

  • It first asks clarifying questions (so it doesn't guess requirements).
  • It uses Chat with Web / Web Research to fetch the latest claims, docs, and updates.
  • It formats the final result in a consistent "decision memo" format every time (so it's ready for internal sharing).
  • It explicitly labels unknowns instead of filling gaps (reduces hallucination risk).

Output quality: what you get (example structure)

  • Executive summary: 3–5 bullets + recommendation (with rationale).
  • Requirements checklist: Security/compliance/SLAs/data handling (pass/partial/unknown).
  • Risk notes: Anything not verified from web sources is flagged as unknown.
  • Next steps: "Ask vendor for SOC2 report / DPA / data retention policy" (actionable).

Why this is better in Beewant context

  • Web Search gives freshness; Agents give repeatability, policy, structure, and guardrails.
  • The agent turns a one-off web summary into a reusable workflow standard that any teammate can run with the same quality.
  • It reduces "prompt skill" dependency: the user can stay simple ("compare A vs B") while the agent enforces the correct process and output format.

2. Example: Generating Images (Without Agents)

Scenario: A marketing manager needs a consistent set of product launch visuals (same brand style, same layout rules, same colors, same typography, same format) for LinkedIn + website hero + blog header.

User workflow (no agent):

  1. User opens a chat and writes a long prompt: "Create a minimalist product launch image. Use our brand colors. Modern style. Add headline text. Make it suitable for LinkedIn."
  2. The user realizes they forgot specs → asks again: "Make it 1200x627 and leave space for logo."
  3. Style is slightly different → asks again: "Use the same template but different headline."
  4. Repeat 10–30 times to get a consistent set.

Typical outcome:

  • Good images, but consistency depends on how well the user remembers to include the same constraints every time.
  • More back-and-forth. More "prompting work" to keep brand alignment consistent.
  • Harder to scale across a team (everyone prompts differently).

Example: Generating images (with an Agent)

Step 1 — Create a "Brand Creative Director" Agent

Agent name: Brand Creative Director

Description: Generates on-brand marketing visuals with consistent style and export-ready specs.

Agent instructions:

  • You are a Brand Creative Director for our company.
  • Your job is to generate images that always follow our brand identity and formatting rules.
  • Before generating, ask only the minimum clarifying questions needed (max 2). If not provided, use safe defaults and state them.
  • Always output image generation prompts with:
    • Visual style (e.g., minimalist, modern, enterprise)
    • Composition rules (clear focal point, whitespace zone for logo)
    • Text rules (short headline, readable contrast, avoid clutter)
    • Platform format rules (LinkedIn, website hero, blog header)
  • Always propose 3 variations:
    • "Clean corporate"
    • "Bold announcement"
    • "Product-focused"
  • Never invent brand colors, fonts, or logo rules. If missing, ask for them or use placeholders and label them clearly.

Step 2 — The user writes a simple request

User prompt (simple): "Create a bird flying for our launch campaign."

What happens (why this is better)

Because the Agent is pre-loaded with rules, the user doesn't need to remember specs every time:

  1. The Agent asks 1–2 questions (only if needed), for example:
    • "Which platform: LinkedIn / website hero / blog header?"
    • "Do you want it photorealistic or illustrated?"
  2. The Agent then produces consistent, production-ready prompts for the Image Generation tool, such as:
    • Version A (Clean corporate): minimalist, lots of whitespace, bird centered, subtle gradient background, reserved logo area, short headline suggestion.
    • Version B (Bold announcement): higher contrast, dynamic motion blur, stronger CTA space.
    • Version C (Product-focused): bird flying above a subtle product silhouette, brand-like geometry.

Outcome:

  • The user can keep asking simple requests ("Now make it a dolphin jumping", "Now make it a rocket", "Now make it a handshake") and still get outputs that match the same campaign style.
  • It scales across the company: anyone can use the same Agent and get the same brand consistency.
  • Faster iterations: fewer re-prompts, less manual "spec remembering".

Example: "Legal AI Note-Taking Vendor Selection" (Web Search vs Tools: Web Search)

Goal

A legal operations team needs a decision memo to choose an AI meeting note-taking tool, covering competitors, pricing, security/compliance, and a recommendation.

Option A — Simple Web Search (Search + Summarize)

User Does: Uses Chat with Web and asks: "Compare AI meeting note-taking tools for legal teams and recommend one."

What happens:

  • The assistant searches the web and produces a narrative summary of the topic.
  • The output quality depends heavily on the prompt (what the user remembered to ask for).
  • If key details are missing (SOC 2, HIPAA, retention policy, whether data is used for training, real pricing), the answer may:
    • Skip those points, or
    • Mention them without strong verification, or
    • Mix primary and secondary sources without clearly separating them.

Typical result: A useful overview, but not reliably "procurement-ready" because it's optimized for summarization, not verification and decision structure.

Option B — Beewant Tools (ReAct: Reasoning + Acting)

User does: Runs the same request with Tools enabled (ReAct). "Create a decision memo to select an AI meeting note-taking tool for legal teams."

What happens:

1. Reasoning (define what must be true):

  • What counts as "legal note-taking" vs generic meeting notes?
  • Which vendor facts must be verified (pricing, compliance, retention, training policy, integrations)?
  • Which sources are acceptable (prefer official pricing/security/docs pages)?

2. Acting (targeted research actions):

  • Pulls official vendor pages for pricing, security, privacy, and product documentation.
  • Cross-checks third-party sources only when needed (e.g., lawsuits, external audits).
  • Collects evidence and flags missing public info.

3. Reasoning (structured synthesis):

  • Converts findings into a consistent memo format and a comparison matrix.
  • Labels any unverified fields as "Not found" instead of guessing.
  • Produces clear next steps (what to request from vendors).

Typical result: A decision-ready memo that's consistent, auditable, and safer for enterprise use.

Why It Matters

  • Web Search is great when you want fast awareness and general context.
  • Tools (ReAct) is what you use when the output must be actionable, verifiable, and ready for leadership/procurement, because the assistant doesn't just fetch information—it follows a reasoning-and-action workflow: define requirements → gather evidence → validate → produce deliverable.

Understood. The correct framing (and the one to use in your documentation) is:

  • Chat = the normal conversational interface (default mode).
  • Tools (ReAct) = the capability layer you can trigger inside chat (web search, image generation, document creation, etc.). It's not "web search only"; it's reasoning + acting across these tools.
  • Agents = a separate layer that sets consistent behavior/brand rules/process, and can use Tools (ReAct) when needed.
  • Collections = your internal knowledge base you can chat with (and it can be used alongside Tools when appropriate).

Chat vs Tools

Chat Only (No Tools):

  • Best for brainstorming, explanations, rewriting, and internal reasoning.
  • Limitation: can't fetch fresh web info, can't generate images/docs as outputs.

Chat + Tools:

  • Best when you need the assistant to do work, not just talk:
    • Web search + validate sources
    • Generate images from prompts
    • Create documents/presentations/spreadsheets
  • Key difference: Tools produces outputs and artifacts, not just text.

Tools vs Agents

Tools without Agents:

  • Powerful, but the user must repeat requirements every time (format, tone, brand rules, compliance rules, etc.).

Agents + Tools:

  • Same power, but now it's consistent and scalable:
    • The agent carries the "how we do things here" (brand voice, formats, rules).
    • The user can ask short prompts and still get the correct result.
